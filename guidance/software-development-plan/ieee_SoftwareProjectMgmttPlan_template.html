Software Project Management Plan

1. Overview

1.1 Project Summary

In the following subsections be concise and avoid duplicating information.  You may reference the Vision statement and business case if appropriate.  Reference specific sections of these documents so the reader doesn't have to search for information.

1.1.1 Project Purpose, Scope, and Objectives

A brief description of the purpose and scope of this project and the project objectives.  You may reference the Vision statement or Project Proposal.

1.1.2 Assumptions and Constraints

A list of assumptions that this plan is based on and any constraints, for example. budget, staff, equipment, schedule that apply to the project.

1.1.3 Project Deliverables

List all the work products to be delivered to the client or acquirer, including target delivery dates. Use a table.

1.2 Evolution of the Project Management Plan

The project management plan may be revised in view of experience and change.  Describe the formal procedure for changing this plan. 

When can it be updated? Who reviews and approves the changes?

2. References

This subsection provides a complete list of all documents referenced elsewhere in this plan. Identify each document by title, report number if applicable, date, and publishing organization. Specify the sources from which the references can be obtained. This information may be provided by reference to an appendix or to another document.

3. Definitions and Acronyms

This subsection provides the definitions of all terms, acronyms, and abbreviations required to properly interpret this plan. This information may be provided by reference to the project’s Glossary.

4. Project Organization

4.1 External Interfaces

Describe how the project interfaces with external groups. For each external group, identify the internal and external contact names.  Customers and professors you report to -- and how you report to them -- are external interfaces.

4.2 Organizational Structure

Describe the organizational structure of the project team, including management and other review authorities. Graphical devices such as organizational charts or diagrams should be used to depict the lines of authority, responsibility, and communication within the project.

4.3 Roles and Responsibilities

Identify the roles, activities, and person responsible.  This is usually given in as a table.  You can organize it by project function (activity group) or by role, but it should cover all activities.  Don't break the work down into detail, since that is given in the "Resource Allocation" section.

5. Management Process

5.1 Start-up Plan

5.1.1 Estimation Plan

Specify the cost and schedule for conducting the project as well as methods, tools, and techniques used to estimate project cost, schedule, resource requirements, and associated confidence levels.

Specify basis of estimation including techniques such as analogy, rule of thumb, or local history and the sources of data.

The cost and schedule estimates may be updated during the project - we haven't covered estimation techniques yet, so your estimates may be crude.  Avoid guestimating.

List when, under what conditions, and how the cost and schedule estimates may be updated.

5.1.2 Staffing Plan

Identify the numbers and type of staff required, including any special skills or experience, scheduled by project phase or iteration.

5.1.3 Resource Acquisition Plan

Describe how you will approach finding and acquiring the staff needed for the project.

For class projects you can't acquire new staff, but you might describe a procedure for finding "consultants", e.g. senior students or instructors.

5.1.4 Training Plan

List any special training project team members will require, with target dates for when this training should be completed.

5.2 Work Plan

Specify the work activities, schedule, resources, and budget details for the software project in the following subsections.

5.2.1 Work Activities

List work activities, down to the task level

· use a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS).  WBS concepts:

·       Hierarchy of deliverables

·       (Optional) work package level at the bottom

5.2.2 Project Schedule

§ Show the scheduling relationships among work activities

§ Show time-sequencing constraints and opportunities for concurrent work

§ Include milestones that can be assessed for achievement using objective indicators

§ Techniques for showing schedule relationships include milestone charts, activity lists, Gantt charts, activity networks, critical path networks, and PERT.

§ Define any important release points and demos.

5.2.3 Resource Allocation

Detailed itemization of the resources allocated to each major work activity in the project work breakdown structure.  This is often shown as a table.

5.2.4 Budget Allocation

Breakdown of the budget to activity and task level

5.3 Control Plan

5.3.1 Requirements Control Plan

Describe your plan for monitoring and controlling changes in requirements.  You should have a single channel for change requests, and a procedure for evaluating whether to accept the request.

5.3.2 Schedule Control Plan

Describe the approach taken to monitor progress against the planned schedule and how to take corrective action when required.

5.3.3 Budget Control Plan

Describe the approach to be taken to monitor spending against the project budget and how to take corrective action when required.  For class projects, "spending" means effort.

5.3.4 Quality Control Plan

Describe the timing and methods to be used to control the quality of the project deliverables and how to take corrective action when required.

5.3.5 Reporting Plan

Describe internal and external reports to be generated, and the frequency and distribution of the reports.

5.3.6 Metrics Collection Plan

You can't monitor and control the project unless you collect some quantitative measurements. What will you measure? How are measurements collected?  Where are they stored?

You should probably at least measure effort, defects, size, and schedule (e.g. tasks performed on-time, early, late).

5.4 Risk Management Plan

Risks must be identified, prioritized, and mitigated.  Describe your plan for this.  The known risks are listed in other documents (SRS, Proposal, or Vision).

5.5 Project Close-out Plan

Describe the activities for orderly completion of the project. Include: staff reassignment, archiving of project materials (artifacts), post-mortem debriefing and reports.

6. Technical Process Plans

6.1 Process Model

Detailed description of the software project life-cycle model to be used

6.2 Methods, Tools, and Techniques

Describe the development methodologies, programming languages, and other notations (e.g. UML, ER) to be used.

Describe the tools and techniques to specify, design, build, test, integrate, document, deliver, modify, and maintain the deliverable and non-deliverable work products.

Describe technical standards, policies, and procedures for development and/or modification of the work products.  For example:

· Business Modeling Guidelines

· User Interfaces Guidelines

· Use-Case-Modeling Guidelines

· Design Guidelines

· Programming Guidelines

· Test Guidelines

· Manual Style guide

6.3 Infrastructure Plan

Technical aspects of hardware and software you will use.  You may also describe physical workspace and procedures for its use and maintenance.

6.4 Product Acceptance Plan

Describe the methods or procedures for project acceptance.

The acceptance criteria are described in another document (e.g. SRS), so reference that instead of duplicating.

7. Supporting Process Plans

7.1 Configuration Management Plan

How will you provide configuration identification, control, status accounting, evaluation, and release management?

Describe the location of work products in the CM system -- probably using a table. You don't need to list the location of each item -- just categorize them.  For example:

Source code

Subversion VCS

http://se.cpe.ku.ac.th/svn/myproject

Project Management docs

Project Wiki

http://se.cpe.ku.ac.th/twiki/myproject

Bugs and issues

Issue tracking system

http://theory.../bugzilla/...

Review Reports

project notebook (hardcopy)

Project Manager

The CM plan should also specify:

§       Convention for naming and organization of files

§       Backup procedure: how are managed items backed up?  Who is responsible for backups?

§       Access control: who has read and write access to managed artifacts?

§       Reconciliation: how will conflicts in versions be resolved (e.g. two people modify the same document and then try to update the CM version)

Change Requests: logging and analysis of change requests, change control board procedures, tracking of changes in progress, and procedures for notifying concerned parties when baselines are first established or later changed

7.2 Verification and Validation Plan

Describe scope, tools, procedures, and techniques for V&V activities.

Verification: requirements traceability, milestone reviews, prototyping, modeling

Validation: testing, demonstration, walkthroughs, code inspection

7.3 Documentation Plan

Describe all forms of documentation, whether or not it is deliverable to the client

7.4 Quality Assurance Plan

Describe how the project will ensure that it meets its quality objectives.  This may include steps to ensure that processes are followed, and other measures in this section (V&V, testing).

7.5 Reviews and Audits Plan

What reviews or audits will be performed?

Reviews may be (a) customer initiated, (b) management initiated, (c) self (author) initiated.

Describe the procedure (process) and output of reviews.

7.6 Problem Resolution Plan

Describe resources, methods, tools, techniques, and procedures to be used in reporting, analyzing, prioritizing, and processing software problem reports generated during the project. The problem resolution plan should indicate the roles of development, configuration management, the change control board, and verification and validation in problem resolution work activities

7.7 Subcontractor Management Plan

See IEEE1058.  Doesn't apply to class projects.

7.8 Process Improvement Plan

Strategy to improve your project processes.

8. Additional Plans

Additional plans if required by contract or regulations.

9. Annexes

Additional material of use to the reader of this plan.
